Where do we fit something like bookmakers match odds to be the priority in the model analysis

Bookmaker odds should not merely be another input alongside team form and statistics. In modern sports betting models, bookmaker odds are often the single most important predictor because they aggregate vast amounts of information, including public sentiment, professional bettors' activity, injuries, team news, weather, and information that your model may not directly observe.

A robust betting intelligence system should treat bookmaker odds as the baseline market model, then search for situations where its own models can identify small but meaningful inefficiencies.

Recommended Model Weighting

Instead of:

Model	Weight
Market Odds	25%
Poisson	25%
Elo	20%
ML	30%

Consider:

Model	Weight
Market Odds	50-60%
Team Strength (Elo)	15%
Poisson Goals Model	10%
Form Model	10%
Machine Learning	10-15%

Reason:

Bookmakers spend millions creating efficient markets.
Professional bettors continuously correct pricing mistakes.
Market odds often outperform standalone predictive models.
Your edge comes from finding where the market is slightly wrong, not completely wrong.
Introduce a Market Efficiency Layer

Before a match is considered a value bet:

Step 1: Calculate Consensus Odds

Gather odds from all bookmakers.

Example:

Bookmaker	Home	Draw	Away
BetWay	2.10	3.30	3.40
BetPawa	2.05	3.25	3.50
PremierBet	2.15	3.20	3.45

Consensus:

Home: 2.10
Draw: 3.25
Away: 3.45
Step 2: Remove Margin

Convert to fair probabilities.

This becomes your "market probability."

Example:

Outcome	Market Probability
Home	46%
Draw	29%
Away	25%
Step 3: Compare Against Internal Models

Suppose:

Outcome	Market	Your Model
Home	46%	52%
Draw	29%	26%
Away	25%	22%

Difference:

Outcome	Edge
Home	+6%
Draw	-3%
Away	-3%

Now investigate why your model disagrees.

An Even Better Feature: Closing Line Value (CLV)

The best long-term betting systems monitor whether their selections beat the closing market.

Example:

You bet:

Over 2.5 @ 2.10

Closing odds:

Over 2.5 @ 1.85

Your model found value before the market fully adjusted.

This is often a stronger indicator of model quality than win rate.
